Community Resource Links

Medical

  • Testing: High-risk Snohomish County residents with COVID-19 symptoms, including those who work in healthcare, public safety or critical infrastructure now have an option for free drive-thru testing. Use this link to sign-up for an appointment and to learn more about testing requirements: Drive-Thru Testing.
  • Medical Facilities: Click the 'Explore' button on the Medical Facility map to the left for locations. 
  • The Snohomish Health District has a wealth of information and guidance on COVID-19. 
  • The Washington Healthcare Access Alliance provides a list of free and charitable clinics.
  • Snohomish County has shared information for mental health and coping during COVID-19.

Food

  • Food Banks: Click on the Food map to the left for all food bank locations in the County. The Snohomish County Foodbank Coalition is a resource with further information for 21 of the food banks in the County. 
  • School Meal Information: While schools are closed, free meals for school aged children are still available at various school and food bank locations throughout the County. Each School District has a specific plan for meals, click the 'Explore' button on the Food Locations map to the left for links to each School district.

  • The Governor has issued a stay home, stay healthy proclamation that is described further below. If you cannot shelter at home, please call 2-1-1 for help.

Stay Home, Stay Healthy Information

Governor Jay Inslee has issued a Stay Home, Stay Healthy order in Washington State. Every Washingtonian is now ordered to stay at home except to perform essential activities, or essential business services. This prohibition shall remain in effect until at least April 6, 2020.

Essential activities are limited to:

  1. Obtaining necessary supplies and services for family or household members and pets, such as groceries, food and supplies for household consumption and use, supplies and equipment needed to work from home, and products necessary to maintain safety, sanitation and essential maintenance of the home or residence. 
  2. Engaging in activities essential for the health and safety of family, household members and pets, including things such as seeking medical or behavioral health or emergency services and obtaining medical supplies or medication.
  3. Caring for a family member, friend, or pet in another household or residence, and to transport a family member, friend or their pet for essential health and safety activities, and to obtain necessary supplies and services.
  4. Engaging in outdoor exercise activities, such as walking, hiking, running or biking, but only if appropriate social distancing practices are used.

Employment in essential business services means an essential employee performing work for an essential business as identified in the “Essential Critical Infrastructure Workers” list, or carrying out minimum basic operations.

Please see the entirety of Proclamation 20-25 for more information. The State also has a helpful guide on what it means to stay at home.
